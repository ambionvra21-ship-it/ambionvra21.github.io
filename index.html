What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a standardized screening metric used globally by health professionals to estimate whether an individual falls within safe body mass boundaries relative to their height. Developed by Lambert Adolphe Quetelet, it offers a quick calculation to flag potential weight risk indicators.

BMI Range Weight Status Category
Below 18.5Underweight
18.5 – 24.9Normal / Healthy Weight
25.0 – 29.9Overweight
30.0 and AboveObesity

Understanding Your Results

While BMI is a highly efficient public health classification utility, it is important to remember it does not map body composition details directly, such as muscle mass versus adipose tissue distributions. Athletes or individuals with heavily developed frames can sometimes present high BMI values without experiencing corresponding cardiometabolic health risks.
